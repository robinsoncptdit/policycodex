Body

The Diocese of Pensacola-Tallahassee shall provide written acknowledgment to any donor making a contribution of $250 or more in a single transaction, in accordance with IRS Publication 1771. The acknowledgment shall state the amount of the contribution, describe any non-cash contributions, and confirm whether any goods or services were provided in exchange.

Acknowledgments shall be issued no later than January 31 of the year following the contribution and shall be retained for a minimum of seven years as part of the diocese's financial records, consistent with the Document Retention Policy (3.2.1).

What you are looking at

Base CSS: Tailwind utility classes plus DaisyUI component classes. In production the build chain is the Tailwind standalone CLI (a single static binary, no Node) running once at install time to emit a minified policycodex.css.

Overlay: The ~30-line block at the top of this file. DaisyUI brand color set in three CSS variables (--p, --pf, --pc), Inter font wired up, and four custom inline components (gate badges, AI pills, gap badges, AI summary accent stripe).

HTMX: Loaded as a single <script> tag. Used only on screen 3 (file upload triggers extraction, panel swaps in place) and screen 4 (Add retention rule appends a row).

Template cost: class-heavy, but the classes are utility-named, not framework-named. Once a designer establishes the patterns once, Django templates reuse them. The visual identity is portable: change --p and the entire app retints.

Diocese customization story: swap brand color via one CSS variable. Swap the typeface by pointing to a different Google Fonts URL. Swap component tone (rounded corners, shadows) via DaisyUI theme variables. No template edits.
