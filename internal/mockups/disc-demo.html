Chapter 3 · Section 1 · Item 1

Funds management: income

Version: 2.1 Owner: CFO Effective: 2014-08-01 Last reviewed: 2024-08-01 Next review: 2025-08-01

Purpose

This policy establishes required and recommended procedures for the receipt, safeguarding, counting, depositing, recording, and donor acknowledgment of all income received by diocesan parishes and schools, emphasizing segregation of duties and internal controls.

Required procedures

All cash receipts shall be counted by two unrelated individuals before deposit. Donor acknowledgments shall be issued within thirty (30) days of receipt of any gift exceeding $250. The Diocesan Finance Officer shall maintain documentation of all extraordinary gifts and pledges in accordance with the Document Retention Policy (3.0).

Segregation of duties

Persons receiving cash shall not also be responsible for recording transactions in the parish accounting system. Persons recording transactions shall not also be responsible for bank reconciliation. The Pastor or his designate shall review the bank reconciliation each month.

Donor acknowledgment

All written acknowledgments shall include:

  • The donor's name and address
  • The amount of the gift, or a description of non-cash gifts
  • A statement of whether goods or services were provided in exchange
  • The signature of an authorized parish or diocesan official

Step 1 of 7 · GitHub repository

Where should your policies live?

PolicyCodex stores every policy as a markdown file in a private GitHub repo owned by your diocese. You control the repo. We just commit on your behalf.

Create a new private repo

We'll create `pt-policy` under the `Diocese-of-Pensacola-Tallahassee` org and apply branch protection on `main`.

Connect to an existing repo

If you already have a policy repo, install the PolicyCodex GitHub App on it and we'll use it.

Your org slug is Diocese-of-Pensacola-Tallahassee. AI suggests the repo name pt-policy (16 of 17 reference dioceses use the abbreviation-policy pattern).
What this step does

PolicyCodex installs a GitHub App on your organization, scoped to one repo. The App lets PolicyCodex open pull requests on your behalf when staff edit a policy. Branch protection is enabled so every change requires a reviewer's approval.

Default branch: main. Required reviewers: 1. Signed commits: on. Force push: blocked.

Why this matters

The AI inventory pass needs to know your diocese's own retention schedule, document-type taxonomy, and naming conventions. Without grounding, it guesses from general records-management norms and the quality drops sharply.

On the PT test corpus, grounding the AI in the retention policy moved the retention-field acceptance rate from 14% to a projected 85%+.

Demo flow for the presentation

The story you walk DISC attendees through:

1. "Here is your policy library today" · open the catalog. Show 164 policies discovered from a SharePoint export, AI-suggested metadata, gate states. Filter by "Missing metadata" to show the gap report.

2. "Here is how a CFO updates a section" · open the edit form for 3.1.1. Show the AI suggestions sidebar grounded in the diocesan retention policy. Click Submit for Review.

3. "Here is what the reviewer sees" · switch to the COO's view. Show the diff, the metadata changes, the comment box. Click Approve.

4. "Here is what parish staff see" · open the public handbook subdomain. Show a stable URL, version metadata, RSS feed, change history.

5. "Here is how install zero went" · the seven-screen wizard. Step 1: GitHub repo. Step 7: point at the Document Retention Policy. Mention that everything in between is opinionated by default and configurable where dioceses have legitimate variation.
