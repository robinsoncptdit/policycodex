Looking at Dify's pricing structure, **"team members" refers to the developers/administrators who BUILD and MANAGE the AI applications**, not the end users who interact with the published apps.

## Dify Team Member vs End User Distinction:

### Team Members (Limited to 3 in Professional):

- **App builders** creating workflows in Dify Studio
- **Administrators** managing LLM settings and API keys
- **Developers** configuring MCP plugins and integrations
- People with access to the Dify dashboard/backend

### End Users (Unlimited in all tiers):

- Diocese staff using the published policy review app
- Department heads uploading policies
- Reviewers providing feedback
- Anyone accessing via the published app URL or API

## For Your Diocese Use Case:

The **Professional tier ($59/month, 3 team members)** could work well because:

1. **Only 2-3 people need builder access**:
    
    - 1 IT administrator (you or team lead)
    - 1 Policy workflow designer
    - 1 backup/training person
2. **Unlimited end users can**:
    
    - Submit policies for review
    - Participate in Seven-Gate approvals
    - Access the policy tracker
    - Use the chat interface
3. **Key Professional features**:
    
    - 5,000 messages/month (sufficient for 200 policies)
    - Managed hosting (no server maintenance)
    - Custom branding for diocese
    - SSO integration (if needed)

## Recommendation:

**Start with Professional** ($59/month) because:

- Faster deployment than self-hosting
- Includes support during critical summer timeline
- Can export and move to Community Edition later if needed
- Test managed service before committing to self-hosting

Your entire diocese staff can use the published apps - only your small tech team needs "team member" seats.
